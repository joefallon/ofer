## Chapter Overview

Culture describes the community, environment, and way of life that shaped your character.

Where lineage answers questions like physical origin and inherited traits, culture answers different questions: Where was your character raised? What kind of world did they grow up in? What customs, habits, languages, and practical skills did they learn from the people around them?

A character's culture is not determined by ancestry. Two characters of the same lineage might come from very different cultures, while characters of many different lineages might share the same culture if they were raised in similar communities.

In OFER, culture is used to represent learned traits rather than inborn ones. These traits reflect upbringing, social environment, training, daily life, and shared customs. A cosmopolitan character, for example, might have learned how to move through crowded cities, speak several languages, and navigate unfamiliar social situations. A frontier character might instead know how to travel rough country, live off the land, and notice danger early.

Culture helps make characters feel grounded in the world. It gives players an easy way to explain what their characters know, how they move through society, and what kinds of communities shaped them before play begins.

When choosing a culture, think about the people who raised your character, the place they called home, and the skills or outlook that daily life would have taught them. Culture is one of the main ways you show who your character is and where they come from.

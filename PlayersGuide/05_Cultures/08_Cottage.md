## Cottage

You were raised in a settled rural community where daily life revolved around the land, the home, and the people who depended on one another. Small farms, herding villages, orchard towns, vineyard country, and other close-knit homesteads all produce cottage communities.

Life in such places teaches patience, practicality, and care for others. You learn how to help with daily work, value comfort after hardship, and find meaning in routine, family, and shared labor. Even if you have left that life behind, you may still carry the habits of the hearth into the wider world.

### Cottage Traits

#### Comforts of Home

As part of a long rest, you can cook a meal, tell stories, or perform some other activity that comforts your allies. Choose a number of creatures who participated in the long rest equal to your proficiency bonus, including yourself if you wish. Those creatures gain temporary hit points equal to twice your proficiency bonus. These temporary hit points last until expended or until you complete your next long rest.

#### Homesteader

You have proficiency in either the Animal Handling or Nature skill.

#### Languages

You know Common and one additional language of your choice.

Typical cottage characters often learn the language of nearby neighbors, trading partners, or long-settled local communities.

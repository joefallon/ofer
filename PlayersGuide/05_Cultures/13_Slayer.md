## Slayer

You were raised in a community that measured itself against dangerous creatures. Your people may have been monster hunters, wardens of threatened lands, protectors of frontier settlements, or hunters of great predators that others feared to face. Some slayer communities travel in packs, some guard known hunting grounds, and others preserve old techniques for bringing down deadly beasts.

Life in such a culture teaches nerve, focus, and respect for lethal prey. You learn how to read tracks and signs, how to recognize the habits of dangerous creatures, and how to project the confidence needed to face things that terrify most people. Even when far from your own people, you carry the habits of the hunt with you.

### Slayer Traits

#### Natural Predator

You have proficiency in the Intimidation skill.

You have advantage on Intimidation checks made to influence Beasts and creatures with the Animal tag.

#### Tracker

When you make a check to locate, spot, or track a creature, you can add your proficiency bonus to the roll. If you already have proficiency in the skill or tool being used, you instead double your proficiency bonus for the roll.

#### Languages

You know Common and one additional language of your choice.

Slayer communities often learn the languages most common among neighboring hunters, frontier peoples, rival predators, or those who dwell near dangerous wild places.

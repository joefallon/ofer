## Wildlands

You were raised in a community shaped by deep wilderness and close familiarity with the creatures that dwell there. Your people may have lived in ancient forests, broad plains, deep marshes, high valleys, or other lands where the rhythms of the wild mattered more than the customs of cities. Some wildlands communities live beside beasts as trusted companions, while others share old understandings with the untamed world around them.

Life in such places teaches patience, observation, and respect for living things. You learn how animals communicate danger, comfort, hunger, fear, and territory. You also learn that force is not always the first answer. In many wildlands communities, survival depends as much on coexistence and instinct as on weapons or walls.

## Wildlands Traits

### Beast Affinity

Using gestures and sounds, you can communicate simple ideas with Beasts and creatures with the Animal tag.

You have advantage on checks made to interact with such creatures.

### Shepherd's Gift

You have proficiency in the Animal Handling skill.

When a Beast or creature with the Animal tag that you can see within 30 feet of you makes an attack roll against you, you can use your reaction to try to calm or redirect it. Make a Wisdom (Animal Handling) check contested by the creature's Wisdom check. On a success, the creature has disadvantage on the attack roll.

Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

### Languages

You know Common and one additional language of your choice.

Wildlands communities often learn the languages most common among neighboring forest peoples, remote clans, fey-touched lands, or others who dwell close to the natural world.

## Nomadic

You were raised in a community that moved from place to place rather than settling in one permanent home. Your people might have followed herds, seasons, trade routes, sacred paths, caravan circuits, or long-standing patterns of migration. Some nomadic communities travel with wagons and animals, some move lightly across great distances, and others return to familiar stopping places year after year.

Life on the move teaches endurance, adaptability, and attention to the world around you. You learn how to travel with what you need, read changing conditions, and endure hardship without losing momentum. Nomadic communities often value memory, cooperation, and practical skill, because survival depends on knowing when to move, where to go, and how to keep the group going together.

### Nomadic Traits

#### Resilient

You have advantage on checks and saving throws made to resist debilitating weather effects, such as those caused by extreme heat or cold.

In addition, when you complete a short rest, you can reduce your exhaustion level by one. Once you use this trait, you can't reduce your exhaustion level in this way again until you complete a long rest.

#### Wayfinder

You have proficiency in the Survival skill.

You also have proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Animal Handling, Nature, or Perception.

#### Languages

You know Common and one additional language of your choice.

Nomadic communities often learn the languages most common among neighboring peoples, trade partners, and other communities they regularly travel among.

## Cosmopolitan

You were raised in a place where many peoples, customs, trades, and languages met together. Great cities, port towns, trade hubs, temple centers, and imperial crossroads all produce cosmopolitan communities.

Life in such places teaches flexibility. You learn how to move through crowds, pick up unfamiliar customs, notice social cues, and adapt quickly when dealing with strangers. A cosmopolitan character is not defined by blood, but by experience in a busy, mixed, connected world.

## Cosmopolitan Traits

### Street Smarts

While you are in a city or other urban environment, you have advantage on ability checks made to avoid getting lost and on checks made to find a particular kind of business or other destination open to the public.

In addition, while you are in such environments, you can't be surprised unless you are asleep or otherwise incapacitated.

### Worldly Wisdom

You have proficiency in the History skill.

When you make a check related to understanding the purpose or significance of a building, rite, object, or custom from a culture you aren't familiar with, you can add your proficiency bonus to the roll. If you already have proficiency in a relevant skill or tool, you instead double your proficiency bonus for the roll.

### Urban Network

In a city or other urban environment, you can spend 1 hour asking questions, following leads, and discreetly checking the right places for news or a specific person.

At the end of that time, make an appropriate ability check. The DC is typically 15 if the person, rumor, or lead is not being hidden, or 20 if it is being actively concealed. On a success, you learn the location of the person, discover a useful lead, or uncover a meaningful clue, as the Narrator determines.

### Languages

You know Common and three additional languages of your choice.

Typical cosmopolitan characters learn the languages most commonly spoken in nearby trade, religious, diplomatic, or immigrant communities.

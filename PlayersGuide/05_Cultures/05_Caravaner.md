## Caravaner

You were raised among people who made the road their home. Your community may have traveled as merchants, migrants, pilgrims, performers, refugees, drovers, or some mixture of all of these. Pack animals, roadside camps, and long miles between safe stops were part of everyday life.

Life in such a community teaches endurance, cooperation, and practical road sense. You learn how to handle animals, keep supplies moving, and adapt when weather, terrain, or trouble disrupts the journey. Caravaner communities often value mutual support, routine, and the ability to make a temporary home wherever the road demands.

## Caravaner Traits

### Caravan Hand

You gain proficiency in the Animal Handling skill.

### Long Hauler

You gain proficiency in the Survival skill.

### Roadwise

You have advantage on ability checks made to secure, organize, conceal, or recover gear, goods, and supplies during travel.

### Languages

You know Common and one additional language of your choice.

Caravaner communities often learn the languages most common among trade partners, neighboring settlements, border stations, or other peoples commonly found along their routes.

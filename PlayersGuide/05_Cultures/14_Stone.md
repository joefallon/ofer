## Stone

You were raised in a community shaped by stone, metal, and the works built to endure. Mountain halls, mining towns, fortified holds, cliff dwellings, quarry settlements, and other hard-built communities all produce stone cultures.

Life in such places teaches patience, discipline, and respect for skilled labor. You learn how things are made, how strong structures are raised, how materials fail, and how craft traditions preserve the work of earlier generations. In many stone communities, practical artistry and readiness for defense go hand in hand, because homes carved from rock or founded on precious resources must often be protected as well as built.

### Stone Traits

#### Ancestral Arts

You gain proficiency with Construction tools.

You double your proficiency bonus for any ability check you make that uses them.

You also gain proficiency with one type of martial weapon of your choice.

#### Eye for Quality

When you make an ability check related to the origin or purpose of an object or structure made of metal or stone, you can add your proficiency bonus to the roll. If you already have proficiency in a relevant skill or tool, you instead double your proficiency bonus for the roll.

#### Languages

You know Common and one additional language of your choice.

Stone communities often teach the languages most common among neighboring miners, crafters, traders, engineers, or long-established mountain and under-earth peoples.

## Starting Equipment

Once you have chosen your class and background, determine what equipment your character starts with.

There are two common ways to do this:

- **take the starting equipment provided by your class and background**
    
- **roll for starting wealth and buy your equipment yourself**
    

Both methods work. The first is faster and simpler. The second gives you more choice.

### Method 1: Take Class and Background Equipment

The easiest way to determine your starting equipment is to take:

- **all starting equipment listed in your class**
    
- **all additional starting equipment listed in your background**
    

Write all of this equipment on your character sheet. Once that is done, you are ready to move on.

This method is usually best for:

- **new players**
    
- **players who want to finish character creation quickly**
    
- **players who do not want to compare lots of gear options**
    

It is also a good choice if you are not yet familiar with the equipment rules.

#### Example

A player creates a fighter whose class grants armor, weapons, and an adventuring pack. Their background also grants a few extra items tied to their former life.

They take:

- **all equipment from the fighter class**
    
- **all equipment from their background**
    

They write it all down and are finished with this step.

### Method 2: Roll Starting Wealth and Buy Equipment

If you want more control over your gear, you can determine starting wealth instead.

To do this:

1. **Roll 5d4 and multiply the total by 10.**
    
2. The result is the number of **gold pieces (gp)** your character starts with.
    
3. Use that gold to buy equipment from the equipment lists.
    
4. Write down everything you buy.
    
5. Keep track of any **unspent gp** on your character sheet.
    

This method is usually best for:

- **players who already know what gear they want**
    
- **players who want more control over their equipment**
    
- **groups that enjoy shopping and customization during character creation**
    

#### Example

A player rolls **5d4** and gets a total of **13**.

- **13 x 10 gp = 130 gp**
    

That character begins with **130 gp** to spend on equipment.

The player buys armor, weapons, a backpack, rations, and other adventuring gear. Any gold they do not spend stays on their character sheet.

### Which Method Should You Use?

If you are not sure, use **class and background equipment**. It is the simplest method and will give your character the gear they need to begin play.

Choose **starting wealth** if you want more control and are comfortable choosing equipment for yourself.

Your GM may decide which method the group uses. If so, follow your GM’s guidance.

Once your equipment is determined, the next step is to fill in the remaining details on your character sheet.

## Step-by-Step Character Creation

This section gives you a quick overview of the character creation process. Follow these steps in order to build your character from start to finish.

If this is your first time, do not worry about making every decision perfectly. Your GM can help explain your options, and many choices will make more sense as you move through the steps.

### Step 0. Gather Materials

Before you begin, make sure you have the basic tools you need to record your choices and fill out your character sheet.

You should have:

- **a pencil**
- **a blank character sheet**
- **scratch paper**
- **dice**

### Step 1. Create a Character Concept

Start by thinking about the kind of character you want to play.

Ask yourself questions like these:

- **Do you want to fight in melee or at range?**
- **Do you want to cast spells?**
- **Do you want to be tough, clever, sneaky, or inspiring?**
- **Do you picture your character as human or something more fantastical?**

You do not need every detail figured out yet. A simple idea is enough to get started.

### Step 2. Choose a Class

Your class is the most important game choice you make during character creation.

Your class determines many of your character’s core abilities, including:

- **the special features you gain**
- **the kinds of weapons, armor, and tools you can use**
- **the skills and saving throws you are best at**
- **how your abilities grow as you level up**

If you already have a character concept, your class is the choice that brings it into the game.

### Step 3. Determine Ability Scores

Every character has six ability scores:

- **Strength**
- **Dexterity**
- **Constitution**
- **Intelligence**
- **Wisdom**
- **Charisma**

These scores describe your character’s natural talents and strongly affect what they do well.

This chapter provides three ways to determine your starting scores:

- **rolling**
- **point-buy**
- **standard array**

After assigning your scores, you will also determine your **ability modifiers**, which are the numbers you will use most often during play.

### Step 4. Choose a Lineage

Your lineage represents your character’s inherited traits.

This includes things like:

- **size**
- **speed**
- **senses**
- **natural adaptations**
- **other inborn traits**

Your lineage helps define what your character is physically like and what special qualities they bring with them into the world.

### Step 5. Choose a Heritage

Your heritage represents your character’s upbringing and cultural background.

This can affect things like:

- **skills**
- **languages**
- **practical knowledge**
- **learned cultural traits**

If lineage helps explain where your character comes from by blood, heritage helps explain how your character was raised.

### Step 6. Choose a Background

Your background represents what your character did before becoming an adventurer.

Your background can provide:

- **skill or tool proficiencies**
- **languages**
- **a talent**
- **starting equipment**
- **an adventuring motivation**

This choice helps connect your character’s past life to the person they are now.

### Step 7. Determine Starting Equipment

Once your main choices are made, determine what equipment your character begins with.

There are two ways to do this:

- **take the starting equipment provided by your class and background**
- **roll for starting wealth and buy equipment yourself**

The first method is faster and simpler. The second gives you more control.

### Step 8. Fill In the Blanks

At this point, the major parts of your character are in place.

Now finish any remaining details, such as:

- **spells**, if your character can cast them
- **derived statistics**
- **your character’s name**
- **appearance**
- **personality**
- **other unfinished parts of the character sheet**

Take a few minutes to review everything and make sure your sheet is complete.

By the end of these steps, your character is ready to play.

## Choose a Class

Your class is the most important rules choice you make during character creation. It defines your character’s core abilities and has a major impact on how your character plays at the table.

You can think of your class as your character’s adventuring role. It shapes the kinds of things your character is best at, the special features they gain, and the way they grow as they gain levels.

Your class gives you **class features**, which are the special abilities that make your character unique. You gain some of these features at 1st level, and you gain more as your character levels up. Each class has a class table that shows when these features are gained.

Your class also gives you **proficiencies**. Proficiencies show what your character has been trained to do. These can include:

- **armor proficiencies**
- **weapon proficiencies**
- **tool proficiencies**
- **skill proficiencies**
- **saving throw proficiencies**

These proficiencies help define both your character’s training and the kinds of challenges they are best prepared to handle.

Choosing a class can also make many of your later decisions easier. Once you know your class, it becomes much simpler to decide:

- **which ability scores matter most**
- **what kind of equipment you want**
- **which background fits your character best**
- **what role your character may fill in the party**

For example:

- A heavily armored warrior usually wants strong physical ability scores and equipment suited for close combat.
- A spellcaster often cares more about mental ability scores and access to spells.
- A nimble or stealthy character may prefer speed, skill, and mobility over raw toughness.

If you already have a character concept in mind, your class is the choice that brings that idea into the game.

If you are not sure which class to choose, look for the class that best matches the kind of character you most want to play. Ask yourself:

- **Do I want to solve problems with weapons, magic, skill, or support?**
- **Do I want a simple class that is easy to learn, or a class with more choices to manage?**
- **Do I want to stand on the front line, fight from a distance, help allies, or control the battlefield?**

There is no single best class. The best choice is the one that sounds fun to you and fits the kind of character you want to play.

Once you choose your class, the next step is to determine your ability scores.

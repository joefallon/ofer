## Create a Character Concept

Before you start choosing rules options, it helps to have a rough idea of the kind of character you want to play.

Your **character concept** is the basic idea behind your character. It does not need to be long or complicated. A few simple ideas are enough to get started.

You might begin with questions like these:

- **Do you want to fight with weapons, cast spells, or do a little of both?**
- **Do you imagine your character on the front line, helping from the rear, or solving problems outside of combat?**
- **Do you want to play someone brave, clever, mysterious, pious, charming, or dangerous?**
- **Do you picture your character as an ordinary person drawn into adventure, or someone born for it?**
- **Do you imagine a human character, or someone from a more fantastical lineage?**

A good concept helps guide the choices you make later. It can make it easier to choose your class, ability scores, lineage, heritage, and background.

Your concept also does not have to be complete right away. You do not need a full life story before play begins. In many cases, a simple idea works best.

For example:

- **a tough warrior who protects others**
- **a clever scholar who studies magic**
- **a wandering hunter who knows the wilds**
- **a charming priest who helps and inspires the party**
- **a sneaky thief who relies on speed and skill**

If the other players are available, talk with them about the kinds of characters they want to play. This can help you make a character who fits well with the rest of the group.

For example:

- If the party already has several strong front-line fighters, you might enjoy playing a healer, scout, or spellcaster.
- If no one is good at talking to people, you might want to play a character who is charming, persuasive, or socially skilled.
- If the party lacks knowledge or magical power, you might decide to play someone scholarly or mystical.

You do not need to build your character around the party, but thinking about the group can help everyone start the game with characters who work well together.

Once you have a basic concept in mind, the next step is to choose a class.

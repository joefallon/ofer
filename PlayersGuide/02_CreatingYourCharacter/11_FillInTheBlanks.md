## Fill In the Blanks

At this point, the biggest character creation choices are done. You know who your character is, where they come from, and what they can do. Now it is time to finish the remaining details on your character sheet.

This step is where you review your character, complete any unfinished sections, and make sure everything is ready for play.

### Choose Spells if Needed

If your character can cast spells, choose any spells, cantrips, or other spell options your class gives you at 1st level.

Be sure to record all relevant spell information on your character sheet, including:

- **spells known**
    
- **spells prepared**, if your class prepares spells
    
- **cantrips**
    
- **spell slots**, if applicable
    
- **spell save DC**
    
- **spell attack modifier**
    

If you are new to spellcasting, do not worry about choosing perfectly. Pick a small set of spells that sound useful and easy to understand.

### Complete Your Numbers

Make sure you have filled in any remaining game statistics that come from your earlier choices.

This may include:

- **ability modifiers**
    
- **maximum hit points**
    
- **armor class**
    
- **initiative**
    
- **speed**
    
- **saving throws**
    
- **skill bonuses**
    
- **proficiency bonus**
    
- **attacks**
    
- **spellcasting numbers**, if applicable
    

Take your time here. A character sheet is much easier to use during play if these numbers are filled in clearly now.

### Record Proficiencies and Features

Make sure you have written down all of your character’s important proficiencies, traits, and special features from their class, lineage, heritage, and background.

This may include:

- **weapon proficiencies**
    
- **armor proficiencies**
    
- **tool proficiencies**
    
- **skill proficiencies**
    
- **languages**
    
- **special traits**
    
- **class features**
    
- **talents**
    

Do not assume you will remember these later. Write them down where you can find them easily during play.

### Add Your Character’s Personal Details

Now give your character the personal details that help bring them to life.

At minimum, decide:

- **your character’s name**
    
- **their appearance**
    
- **their basic personality**
    
- **their background details**
    
- **their motivation for adventuring**
    

You do not need to write a long backstory unless you want to. A few clear details are often enough to begin.

Helpful questions include:

- **What does your character look like?**
    
- **How do they usually behave?**
    
- **What do they care about most?**
    
- **Why did they become an adventurer?**
    
- **What are they hoping to gain, protect, or prove?**
    

### Leave Room to Grow

You do not need to decide everything right away.

It is perfectly fine to begin play with only a simple idea of who your character is. Many characters become more interesting over time as you play them. After a few sessions, you may have a much clearer sense of their voice, habits, beliefs, and goals.

A strong beginning matters more than a perfect beginning.

### Review Your Sheet

Before moving on, look over your character sheet one more time and check for any blank spaces or missing details.

Make sure your sheet is:

- **complete enough to use at the table**
    
- **clear and easy to read**
    
- **organized so you can find important information quickly**
    

Once you have filled in the blanks, the last step is the final checklist.

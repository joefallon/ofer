## Goliath

### Overview

Goliaths are towering people marked by giant ancestry. Broad-shouldered, powerful, and difficult to overlook, they often carry themselves with the confidence of those born to endure harsh conditions and great trials. Some are wanderers from high places, some are disciplined competitors, and some seem almost mythic in the way giant power shows itself through them.

A goliath character is a strong choice for players who want a lineage that feels mighty, mobile, and larger than life.

### Lineage Traits

Your goliath character has the following traits.

#### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

#### Size

You are Medium.

#### Speed

Your walking speed is 35 feet.

#### Powerful Build

You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

You also have advantage on ability checks made to end the Grappled condition.

### Giant Ancestry

You are descended from giants. Choose one of the following ancestral boons: Cloud’s Jaunt, Fire’s Burn, Frost’s Chill, Hill’s Tumble, Stone’s Endurance, or Storm’s Thunder.

You can use your chosen boon a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

#### Cloud’s Jaunt

You inherit a trace of cloud giant magic.

**Teleport**

As a bonus action, you magically teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see.

#### Fire’s Burn

Your strikes carry the heat of giant-forged flame.

**Burning Strike**

When you hit a target with an attack roll and deal damage to it, you can deal an extra 1d10 fire damage to that target.

#### Frost’s Chill

Your blows carry numbing giant cold.

**Chilling Strike**

When you hit a target with an attack roll and deal damage to it, you can deal an extra 1d6 cold damage to that target and reduce its speed by 10 feet until the start of your next turn.

#### Hill’s Tumble

You strike with the force of an avalanche.

**Toppling Blow**

When you hit a Large or smaller creature with an attack roll and deal damage to it, you can knock that creature prone.

#### Stone’s Endurance

You can draw on the stubborn durability of stone giants.

**Endure Harm**

When you take damage, you can use your reaction to roll 1d12 and add your Constitution modifier to the result, reducing the damage by that total.

#### Storm’s Thunder

You answer pain with a crack of giant thunder.

**Thunderous Reprisal**

When a creature within 60 feet of you deals damage to you, you can use your reaction to deal 1d8 thunder damage to that creature.

### Playing a Goliath

Goliaths often suit characters defined by strength, endurance, ambition, and visible physical presence. Some are driven by challenge, competition, and the need to prove themselves. Others are thoughtful, disciplined, and measured, carrying their great strength with calm restraint. One goliath might be a fearless climber and explorer, another a solemn guardian, another a proud warrior, and another a quiet traveler whose giant heritage appears only when pressure rises.

When playing a goliath, think about questions like these:

- How does your giant ancestry show itself in your appearance or manner?
- Do you seek out hardship and challenge, or endure them because life has demanded it of you?
- Is your strength a source of pride, responsibility, or isolation?
- Does your power feel inherited, earned, or both?

Goliaths are often at their best when they feel imposing, capable, and impossible to ignore.

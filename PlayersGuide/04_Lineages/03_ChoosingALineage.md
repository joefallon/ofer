## Choosing a Lineage

Choose a lineage by starting with the kind of character you want to play at the table. Think first about fantasy, tone, and play experience. Do you want your character to feel sturdy, swift, adaptable, imposing, uncanny, subtle, or something else entirely? Your lineage should support the kind of presence and identity you want your character to have from the beginning.

A good lineage choice should make your character easier to imagine. It helps shape appearance, physical presence, natural talents, and the way others might see you in the world. That does not mean it decides your personality or role for you. Instead, it gives you a strong starting point that can support the character idea already forming in your mind.

As you look through the lineages in this chapter, pay attention to three things: theme, traits, and fit.

**Theme** is the fantasy the lineage offers. A lineage should feel exciting before you ever look at the mechanics. If the idea of the lineage does not interest you, the traits alone usually will not make it the right choice.

**Traits** are the game benefits the lineage provides. Look for traits that support the way you want to play. A lineage with mobility features may suit a character who wants to stay in motion. A lineage with resilience may fit a character who expects hardship. A lineage with flexible options may work well if you want room to adapt as the character grows.

**Fit** is how well the lineage works with the rest of your character concept. Think about how it combines with your class, background, and personality. The goal is not to find a perfect mechanical match. The goal is to create a character that feels coherent, fun, and easy to play.

Do not assume that the most powerful-looking lineage is the best choice. A lineage is successful when it helps your character feel vivid and distinct in play. The best option is usually the one that gives you a clear picture of who your character is and makes you excited to see them in action.

If you are unsure, choose the lineage that most naturally fits your first strong idea. If several appeal to you, pick the one whose traits you expect to notice and enjoy most often during play.

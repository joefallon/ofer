## Lineage Traits and Terminology

Each lineage entry in this chapter follows the same general structure. This section explains the terms that appear in those entries so you can read them quickly and understand what they mean during character creation.

### Creature Type

Your creature type describes the broad kind of being you are for rules purposes. Most player characters in this chapter are **Humanoids**. This matters only when another rule specifically refers to creature type.

### Size

Your size describes the amount of space your character naturally occupies. Most player characters are **Small** or **Medium**.

Size affects how some rules interact with you. It can matter for carrying, grappling, movement through tight spaces, and the kinds of mounts or equipment that make sense for your character.

If a lineage lets you choose your size, make that choice when you select the lineage.

### Speed

Your speed is how far you can move on your turn under normal conditions. A speed of 30 feet means you can usually move up to 30 feet with your movement on your turn.

Some traits, spells, conditions, or hazards can increase or reduce your speed.

### Proficiency

If a lineage says you gain proficiency with a skill, tool, weapon, or saving throw, you add your proficiency bonus to rolls that use that proficiency.

Lineage proficiencies represent natural aptitude, inherited training, or abilities that are commonly associated with your people.

### Special Traits

Many lineages grant special traits beyond the basics. These are the features that make one lineage feel different from another in play.

Special traits may include things like:

- darkvision or other special senses
    
- resistance to a type of damage
    
- advantage on certain saving throws
    
- extra movement options
    
- a bonus skill or tool proficiency
    
- a limited-use special ability
    
- an unusual feature tied to the lineage’s identity
    

These traits are always explained in the lineage entry itself.

### Limited-Use Traits

Some lineage traits can only be used a certain number of times before you must rest. When a trait says it can be used **once per long rest**, you regain the ability to use it after finishing a long rest. If it says **once per short or long rest**, a short rest is enough to restore it.

If a trait does not list a limit, you can usually use it whenever its conditions are met.

### Choices Within a Lineage

Some lineages include a choice inside the lineage entry itself. This might represent different gifts, paths, knacks, or other forms of variation within the same broad people.

When a lineage tells you to choose one option, you make that choice when you select the lineage. You gain all traits listed under the option you choose.

These internal choices help keep a lineage recognizable while still allowing meaningful variation between characters of the same lineage.

### Lineage and Character Identity

A lineage gives your character a foundation, but it does not determine everything about them. A lineage entry tells you what traits you gain, not what personality you must have, what role you must fill, or what story you must follow.

Use lineage traits as tools that support your character concept. They should help your character feel distinct in play without locking you into a narrow identity.

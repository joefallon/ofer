## Dwarf

### Overview

Dwarves are hardy, disciplined, and enduring. They are known for their resilience, their skill with stone and craft, and their refusal to yield when hardship closes in. Whether they are builders, miners, smiths, guardians, or wanderers carrying old traditions into new lands, dwarves are often defined by patience, toughness, and an unshakable sense of purpose.

A dwarf character is a strong choice for players who want a lineage that feels durable, dependable, and deeply rooted.

### Lineage Traits

Your dwarf character has the following traits.

#### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

#### Size

You are Medium.

#### Speed

Your walking speed is 25 feet. Your speed is not reduced by wearing heavy armor.

#### Darkvision

You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet.

#### Dwarven Resilience

You have resistance to poison damage, and you have advantage on saving throws made to avoid or end the Poisoned condition.

#### Dwarven Toughness

Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 again whenever you gain a level.

#### Creator’s Blessing

You gain proficiency with one of the following: brewer’s supplies, mason’s tools, or smith’s tools.

During a long rest, you can use the chosen tools for light crafting or maintenance instead of sleeping and still gain the full benefits of the rest.

#### Stonecunning

As a bonus action, you gain tremorsense with a range of 60 feet for 10 minutes. To use this tremorsense, you must be standing on stone or touching a stone surface. The stone can be natural or worked.

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

### Dwarven Gift

Choose one of the following Dwarven Gifts: Dwarven Stability or Dwarven Endurance. You gain all traits listed under the gift you choose.

#### Dwarven Stability

You are especially hard to move, topple, or throw off balance.

**Sure-Footed**

You have advantage on saving throws made to avoid being knocked prone.

**Set Against the Push**

You have advantage on saving throws and ability checks made to resist being shoved, pushed, or forcibly moved against your will.

#### Dwarven Endurance

You can draw on deep reserves of toughness when danger closes in.

**Stand Firm**

As a bonus action, you gain temporary hit points equal to 1d10 + your level. These temporary hit points last for 1 minute.

Once you use this trait, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Poison-Hardened**

You have advantage on saving throws made against poison.

### Playing a Dwarf

Dwarves suit characters defined by resolve, patience, discipline, and staying power. Some are deeply tied to family, clan, guild, or tradition, while others carry the strengths of their people into lives of exile, wandering, trade, war, or discovery. One dwarf might be a stern mason and engineer, another a cheerful brewer, another a grim tunnel-fighter, and another a thoughtful scholar of ancient ruins.

When playing a dwarf, think about questions like these:

- What work, craft, or tradition shaped you?
- Is your toughness a source of pride, habit, or necessity?
- Do you feel called to preserve the old ways, or to carry dwarven strengths into a changing world?
- When pressure rises, do you become steady, stubborn, or fierce?

Dwarves are often at their best when they feel grounded, capable, and difficult to break.

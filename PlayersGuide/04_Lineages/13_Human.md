## Human

### Overview

Humans are adaptable, ambitious, and endlessly varied. No single way of life defines them. Some become explorers, scholars, crafters, soldiers, wanderers, rulers, or survivors. What humans lack in innate specialization, they make up for with determination, flexibility, and the drive to learn quickly.

A human character is a strong choice for players who want a lineage that feels open-ended, practical, and highly customizable.

### Lineage Traits

Your human character has the following traits.

#### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

#### Size

You are Medium or Small. Choose your size when you select this lineage.

#### Speed

Your walking speed is 30 feet.

#### Adaptable

You gain proficiency in one skill of your choice.

#### Driven

Human effort and determination often carry them through difficult moments. Once per long rest, when you make an ability check, attack roll, or saving throw, you can roll a d4 and add the number rolled to the result. You can decide to use this trait after rolling the d20, but before the outcome is determined.

### Human Gift

Choose one of the following Human Gifts (Diehard Survivor, Ingenious Focus, or Spirited Traveler). You gain all traits listed under the gift you choose.

#### Diehard Survivor 

You are stubborn, durable, and hard to put down.

**Hard to Kill**

When you make a death saving throw, you can roll a d4 and add it to the roll. After you use this benefit, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Enduring**

You have advantage on saving throws made to resist exhaustion caused by forced march, harsh travel, or environmental hardship.

#### Ingenious Focus

You are disciplined, methodical, and exceptionally good at applying what you know.

**Resident Expert**

Choose one skill you are proficient in or one set of tools you are proficient with. Whenever you make a check using the chosen proficiency, you can treat a d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10.

**Steady Concentration**

When you fail a Constitution saving throw made to maintain concentration, you can reroll it. After you use this benefit, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

#### Spirited Traveler 

You are swift, capable, and at home on the road.

**Desperate Dash**

When you take the Dash action, opportunity attacks provoked by that movement are made with disadvantage. After you use this benefit, you cannot use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

**Road-Hardened**

You have advantage on ability checks and saving throws made to endure long journeys, forced travel, or overland hazards.

### Playing a Human

Humans fit almost any character concept. They work especially well for characters defined less by inherited power and more by training, grit, curiosity, ambition, or personal choice.

When playing a human, think about questions like these:

- What drives you to adventure when others would stay home?
- Are you known more for talent, determination, or adaptability?
- Did you survive hardship, master a craft, or build yourself through relentless effort?
- What does your character believe people can become through will, learning, and experience?

Humans are often at their best when they are played as people in motion: striving, changing, building, learning, and pushing forward.

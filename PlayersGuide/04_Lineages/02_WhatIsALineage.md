## What Is a Lineage?

A lineage represents the people your character comes from and the inherited traits that come with that heritage. It describes the broad physical and innate qualities your character was born with or shaped by through ancestry. A lineage helps define what your character is, but it does not fully define who your character is.

Your lineage gives you a starting foundation during character creation. It may affect your size, speed, senses, resilience, natural talents, or other special traits. These features help distinguish one lineage from another in play. An elf and a dwarf may both become warriors, but they do not begin from exactly the same foundation. A human and a tiefling may both become scholars, but the gifts and qualities tied to their lineages can still make them feel very different at the table.

In OFER, a lineage is only one part of a character. Your class, background, skills, goals, personality, and choices matter just as much, and often more. Two characters of the same lineage can have entirely different lives, values, and roles in an adventuring party. Lineage gives you a frame to build from, not a script you are forced to follow.

Lineages are meant to support fantasy, not limit it. They help express familiar fantasy peoples such as dwarves, elves, humans, halflings, and others in a way that is clear, flavorful, and useful in play. Each lineage should feel recognizable, but flexible enough to support many different character concepts.

Some lineages are simple and direct. Others include an internal choice, such as a gift, knack, or lineage-specific path, that lets you further shape what that lineage looks like for your character. These choices are still part of your lineage, but they help show that even within the same broad people, individuals can differ in meaningful ways.

When you choose a lineage, you are choosing part of your character’s origin and nature. You are not choosing their destiny. What your character becomes will depend on the decisions you make after that choice.

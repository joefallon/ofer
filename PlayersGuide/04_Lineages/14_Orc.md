## Orc

### Overview

Orcs are powerful, enduring, and difficult to break. They are often associated with hard lives, harsh lands, and the strength needed to survive both. Some orcs are fierce warriors, some are determined wanderers, some are spiritual guardians of old traditions, and some are builders, hunters, or leaders shaped by struggle rather than defeated by it. What unites them is force of body, force of will, and a refusal to give in easily.

An orc character is a strong choice for players who want a lineage that feels relentless, physical, and resilient under pressure.

### Lineage Traits

Your orc character has the following traits.

#### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

#### Size

You are Medium.

#### Speed

Your walking speed is 30 feet.

#### Darkvision

You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet.

#### Adrenaline Rush

You can take the Dash action as a bonus action. When you do so, you gain a number of temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a short or long rest.

#### Heavy Lifter

You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

#### Relentless Endurance

When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead.

Once you use this trait, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

### Orc Gift

Choose one of the following Orc Gifts: Just Like Home, Ancestral Blessing, or Battle Fury. You gain all traits listed under the gift you choose.

#### Just Like Home

You are especially adapted to one kind of harsh homeland.

**Homeland**

Choose one type of terrain: arctic, desert, mountain, or swamp.

**Harsh Terrain Adaptation**

You ignore naturally created difficult terrain of your chosen type.

**Homeland Resilience**

You gain resistance to one damage type based on your chosen terrain:

- **Arctic:** Cold
- **Desert:** Fire
- **Mountain:** Lightning
- **Swamp:** Poison

**Homeland Hunter**

You have advantage on Survival checks made in your chosen terrain.

#### Ancestral Blessing

Spiritual strength and old protection linger around you.

**Divine Protection**

You have resistance to radiant damage.

**Touch of Divinity**

You know the Resistance cantrip.

Once per long rest, you can cast Shield without using a spell slot.

Choose Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma as your spellcasting ability for these spells when you select this gift.

#### Battle Fury

Your power shows itself most clearly when violence begins.

**Mighty Attacks**

When you score a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, roll one of the weapon’s damage dice one additional time and add it to the damage.

**Stalwart Recovery**

When an effect allows you to make a saving throw at the end of each of your turns to end it, you can also make that saving throw at the start of your turn. Once you benefit from this trait, you cannot do so again until you finish a short or long rest.

### Playing an Orc

Orcs often suit characters defined by intensity, resolve, and visible strength, but that strength can take many forms. One orc may be a tireless warrior, another a practical survivor shaped by hard country, another a solemn keeper of old rites, and another a disciplined protector who knows exactly how much weight they can carry and how much hardship they can endure.

When playing an orc, think about questions like these:

- What kind of hardship shaped your character’s strength?
- Do you meet pressure with fury, patience, endurance, or discipline?
- Is your power something others fear, admire, misunderstand, or depend on?
- What do you refuse to surrender, even when the world demands it?

Orcs are often at their best when they feel forceful, grounded, and impossible to keep down.

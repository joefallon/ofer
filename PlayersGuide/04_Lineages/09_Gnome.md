## Gnome

### Overview

Gnomes are small, clever, and touched by subtle magic. They are often associated with wit, curiosity, illusion, invention, and an instinct for slipping past dangers that would overwhelm larger folk. Some gnomes are tied to deep woods, hidden burrows, and talking beasts, while others are makers, tinkerers, and patient crafters of strange little wonders.

A gnome character is a strong choice for players who want a lineage that feels clever, magical, elusive, and full of personality.

### Lineage Traits

Your gnome character has the following traits.

#### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

#### Size

You are Small.

#### Speed

Your walking speed is 30 feet.

#### Darkvision

You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet.

#### Gnomish Cunning

You have advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

#### Gnomish Magic

You know the Minor Illusion cantrip. Choose Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma as your spellcasting ability for this spell when you select this lineage.

### Gnome Gift

Choose one of the following Gnome Gifts: Forest Gnome, Rock Gnome, or Into Mist. You gain all traits listed under the gift you choose.

#### Forest Gnome

You are especially attuned to hidden places, small creatures, and quiet woodland magic.

**Speak with Small Beasts**

You always have Speak with Animals prepared. You can cast it without a spell slot a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. This version of the spell only lets you communicate with Small or smaller beasts.

You can also cast the spell using any spell slots you have.

**Woodland Subtlety**

You gain proficiency in either Nature, Perception, or Survival. Choose one when you select this gift.

#### Rock Gnome

You are an ingenious maker of small devices, repairs, and practical wonders.

**Tinker’s Knack**

You know the Mending cantrip.

**Clockwork Device**

By spending 10 minutes of work, you can create a Tiny clockwork device, such as a toy, fire starter, noisemaker, or music box. When you create it, choose one simple sensory or utility effect it can produce, such as lighting or extinguishing a small flame, creating a brief sound, or performing a minor harmless sensory trick. A creature can activate the device with a touch as a bonus action.

You can have up to three such devices at one time. A device falls apart after 8 hours, or when you dismantle it with a touch.

#### Into Mist

You are unusually elusive, vanishing just when danger closes around you.

**Fade Away**

Once per short or long rest, you can turn invisible until the end of your next turn. You can do this as a bonus action on your turn, or as a reaction immediately after you take damage.

The invisibility ends early if you attack, deal damage, or cast a spell.

### Playing a Gnome

Gnomes often suit characters defined by curiosity, wit, inventiveness, mischief, or quiet confidence. Some are playful tricksters, others are patient craftspeople, and others seem harmless until they reveal how sharp, stubborn, or uncanny they really are. One gnome might be a forest scout who talks to every bird in the hedgerow, another a meticulous tinker surrounded by strange devices, and another a soft-spoken illusionist who is never where an enemy expects.

When playing a gnome, think about questions like these:

- Is your cleverness practical, playful, scholarly, or strange?
- Do you use magic to entertain, to deceive, to solve problems, or to survive?
- Are you more at home in hidden natural places, in workshops, or in bustling communities?
- Do others underestimate you, and if so, how do you use that to your advantage?

Gnomes are often at their best when they feel inventive, surprising, and just a little hard to catch.

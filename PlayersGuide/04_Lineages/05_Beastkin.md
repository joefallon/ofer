## Beastkin

### Overview

Beastkin are people marked by visible animal traits such as fur, feathers, scales, horns, claws, fangs, hooves, tails, or wings. Some resemble a particular kind of beast, while others show a mix of animal features shaped by ancestry, environment, or family line. Beastkin are often quick to act, deeply perceptive, and closely tied to the strengths of the natural world.

A beastkin character is a strong choice for players who want a lineage that feels primal, flexible, and physically distinctive.

### Lineage Traits

Your beastkin character has the following traits.

#### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

#### Size

You are Medium or Small. Choose your size when you select this lineage.

#### Speed

Your walking speed is 30 feet.

#### Animal Instinct

You gain proficiency in either Perception or Survival. Choose one when you select this lineage.

#### Natural Weapons

You have claws, horns, hooves, fangs, spines, or a similar natural weapon. Choose the form your natural weapon takes when you select this lineage. Also choose whether it deals bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage.

Your natural weapon can be used to make unarmed strikes. When you hit with it, your unarmed strike deals 1d6 + your Strength or Dexterity modifier damage, instead of the normal damage for an unarmed strike. Choose whether this damage uses Strength or Dexterity when you select this lineage.

### Natural Adaptation

Choose one of the following Natural Adaptations: Avian, Agile, Aquatic, or Sturdy. You gain all traits listed under the adaptation you choose.

#### Avian

Leathery, feathery, or gossamer wings mark your lineage.

**Flight**

You have a flying speed equal to your walking speed. You cannot use this flying speed while wearing medium or heavy armor.

**Burdened Flight**

If you are carrying a heavy load, or carrying a creature of your size or larger, your flying speed is halved.

#### Agile

Sharp claws, cloven hooves, powerful limbs, reversed joints, or gripping pads help you move with unusual control.

**Climber**

You have a climbing speed equal to your walking speed.

**Sure-Footed**

You have advantage on saving throws made to avoid being knocked prone.

#### Aquatic

Scales, fins, webbed limbs, sleek fur, or other water-adapted features shape your body.

**Swimmer**

You have a swimming speed equal to your walking speed.

**Deep Breath**

You can hold your breath for up to 20 minutes.

#### Sturdy

Thick hide, dense muscle, powerful limbs, or a heavy frame make you especially durable.

**Natural Armor**

When you are not wearing armor, your Armor Class equals 13 + your Dexterity modifier.

**Powerful Build**

You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

### Playing a Beastkin

Beastkin can support a wide range of character ideas. Some lean into their animal traits and instincts, while others define themselves by culture, training, ambition, or personal discipline. One beastkin might be a keen-eyed scout, another a ferocious warrior, another a river guide, and another a soft-spoken scholar with the features of a hawk, bear, cat, or serpent.

When playing a beastkin, think about questions like these:

- What animal traits are most visible in your appearance?
- Do you embrace those traits, or try to rise above how others see you?
- Are your instincts a source of pride, discomfort, or mystery?
- Does your character feel more at home in settlements, in the wild, or between both worlds?

Beastkin are often at their best when they feel vivid, physical, and immediately memorable at the table.

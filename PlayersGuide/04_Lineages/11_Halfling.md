## Halfling

### Overview

Halflings are small, nimble, and remarkably hard to keep down. They are known for luck, quick feet, quiet courage, and an ability to slip through danger that larger folk often underestimate. Some halflings are cheerful wanderers, some are close-knit homebodies, and some survive through stubborn grit and uncanny timing that seems almost supernatural.

A halfling character is a strong choice for players who want a lineage that feels agile, lucky, stealthy, and resilient in surprising ways.

### Lineage Traits

Your halfling character has the following traits.

#### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

#### Size

You are Small.

#### Speed

Your walking speed is 30 feet.

#### Brave

You have advantage on saving throws made to avoid or end the Frightened condition.

#### Halfling Nimbleness

You can move through the space of any creature that is at least one size larger than you, but you cannot stop there.

#### Luck

When you roll a 1 on the d20 for an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can reroll the die, and you must use the new roll.

#### Naturally Stealthy

You can take the Hide action even when you are obscured only by a creature that is at least one size larger than you.

### Halfling Gift

Choose one of the following Halfling Gifts: Burrowing Claws, Tuft Feet, or Twilight-Touched. You gain all traits listed under the gift you choose.

#### Burrowing Claws

You are adapted for digging, scrambling, and surviving close to the earth.

**Burrow**

You have a burrowing speed of 10 feet. You can use this speed to move through nonmagical sand, loose earth, loamy soil, mud, or snow, but not solid rock.

You do not normally leave a usable tunnel behind. By moving slowly and taking extra care, you can create a rough tunnel through earth, soil, or snow.

**Claws**

Your nails are strong enough to serve as natural weapons. You can use them to make unarmed strikes. When you hit with them, your unarmed strike deals 1d4 + your Strength modifier slashing damage instead of the normal damage for an unarmed strike.

#### Tuft Feet

You are unusually steady and sure-footed, with a talent for crossing rough ground.

**Big Feet**

You have advantage on ability checks and saving throws made to resist being knocked prone.

**Thick Soles**

You ignore damage from sharp ground hazards such as caltrops, broken glass, or similar terrain dangers, and such hazards do not count as difficult terrain for you.

Other kinds of difficult terrain reduce your speed by 5 feet instead of costing extra movement.

#### Twilight-Touched

A subtle strangeness lingers around you, like the hush of evening or the edge of a dream.

**Darkvision**

You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet.

**Telepathy**

You can speak telepathically to one creature you can see within 30 feet of you. The creature does not gain the ability to respond telepathically, but it can understand you if you share a language.

### Playing a Halfling

Halflings often suit characters defined by quickness, nerve, warmth, humility, or surprising resolve. Some rely on luck and timing, others on subtle movement and social grace, and others on a refusal to panic when larger and more dangerous things loom over them. One halfling might be a wandering burglar, another a cheerful scout, another a stubborn tunnel-runner, and another a quiet traveler with an uncanny twilight gift.

When playing a halfling, think about questions like these:

- Is your courage quiet and steady, or bold and irrepressible?
- Do you rely more on luck, skill, or the fact that others overlook you?
- Are you tied to a close community, or always half-ready to wander?
- When danger rises, do you disappear, endure, or dart straight through it?

Halflings are often at their best when they feel quick, grounded, and far harder to corner than anyone expects.

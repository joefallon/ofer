## Awarding XP

In an XP-based campaign, characters advance by earning experience points during play.

Each challenge overcome adds to the party's progress. A dangerous fight, a clever solution, a risky expedition, or another meaningful obstacle can move the characters closer to their next level. Over time, those victories add up.

XP gives advancement a visible shape. Players can see progress building session by session, and every hard-won success feels like it matters.

## When XP Is Awarded

In most campaigns that use XP, the GM awards it at the end of a session.

This keeps the game moving during play and lets the GM total up everything the party overcame before announcing the reward. Some groups may handle XP differently, but the important part is consistency. Players should understand when XP is awarded and how it is being tracked.

## How XP Works

Each character has an XP total.

When that total reaches the amount required for the next level, the character levels up. You do not spend XP to gain levels, and you do not reset your XP total after leveling. Your XP keeps increasing over the course of the campaign.

That last point matters.

XP is cumulative. It is a running total, not a set of points that gets emptied each time you advance.

## Reading the Table

The XP listed for each level is the total XP required to be that level.

For example:

- a character becomes 2nd level at 300 XP
- a character becomes 3rd level at 900 XP
- a character becomes 5th level at 6,500 XP

You do not subtract the previous level's total. You compare your current total XP to the table and see what level it qualifies for.

## Example: Cumulative XP

Lysa begins the campaign at 1st level with 0 XP.

After her first few adventures, she reaches 300 XP and becomes 2nd level.

Later, her total rises to 900 XP and she becomes 3rd level.

She does not reset to 0 XP when she reaches 2nd level. Her total keeps climbing from 300 to 900 and beyond.

## Example: Close to the Next Level

Dain is 4th level and currently has 5,900 XP.

The table shows that 5th level begins at 6,500 XP.

That means Dain needs 600 more XP to reach 5th level.

This is one of the strengths of XP-based advancement: players can always see how close the next level is.

## What Counts for XP?

The GM decides what earns XP.

In many campaigns, XP is awarded for overcoming challenges. Combat is an obvious example, but it does not have to be the only one. Outsmarting a threat, surviving a dangerous expedition, solving a major problem, or achieving a difficult objective can all represent meaningful progress.

What matters most is that the GM applies the method clearly and consistently, so players understand what kinds of actions move the campaign forward.

## Why Some Groups Like XP

XP-based advancement has a very specific feel.

It rewards steady effort. Even when a session ends without a dramatic story breakthrough, the party can still see that progress was made. The number rises, the next level draws closer, and the campaign feels like it is always moving.

It also gives players a strong sense of momentum. Every dangerous choice and every challenge overcome contributes to something tangible.

## A Good Table Habit

If your group uses XP, keep it visible.

Write it on the character sheet, note it at the end of each session, and update it promptly when the GM announces awards. Advancement is more satisfying when players can see the climb happening.

## Quick Reference

In an XP-based campaign:

- the GM awards XP
- XP is usually awarded after the session
- your XP total is cumulative
- you level up when your total XP reaches the next threshold on the Level Advancement table

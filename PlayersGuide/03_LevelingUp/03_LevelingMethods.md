## Leveling Methods

Heroes grow through experience, but not every group tracks that growth the same way.

Some campaigns measure progress one challenge at a time, counting every hard-fought victory and narrow escape. Others treat advancement as part of the story, with characters leveling up when they reach important goals.

Both approaches work well. The right choice depends on the kind of campaign your group wants to play.

## Who Decides?

The GM decides which leveling method the campaign uses.

That choice should be clear to everyone at the table from the start. Players make different decisions when they know how advancement works. In an XP-based game, every challenge overcome may feel like part of the climb. In a milestone game, players may focus more on major objectives, discoveries, and turning points in the story.

## The Two Main Methods

This game supports two main ways to advance in level:

### Awarding XP

Characters gain experience points, usually when the session ends and the GM totals up what the party overcame. When a character reaches the required XP threshold for the next level, that character levels up.

This method gives players a visible progress track. You can watch the numbers rise and know exactly how close the next level is.

### Tracking Milestones

Characters level up when they achieve a major goal or reach an important point in the campaign, as determined by the GM.

This method keeps advancement tied closely to the story. Instead of counting points, the group advances when they accomplish something that matters.

## Neither Method Is More Correct

XP and milestones are both valid tools.

XP works especially well when the campaign features clear challenges, dangerous exploration, or a steady stream of encounters to overcome. It gives advancement a measured, incremental feel.

Milestones work especially well when the campaign is driven by missions, revelations, political shifts, personal goals, or other major story beats. It gives advancement a more dramatic and narrative feel.

Many groups prefer one strongly over the other, but neither is the "right" choice for every table.

## Example: Same Adventure, Two Approaches

Imagine the party clears a haunted watchtower, survives its traps, defeats the undead inside, and recovers a lost relic.

In an XP-based campaign, the GM might total the threats overcome and award XP at the end of the session.

In a milestone campaign, the GM might decide that reclaiming the watchtower was a major objective and award a level when the goal is completed.

The adventure is the same. The difference is how the group tracks the result.

## What Players Should Know

Whatever method your group uses, leveling should feel fair, understandable, and satisfying.

Players should know:

- whether the campaign uses XP or milestones
- when advancement is usually announced
- whether leveling happens immediately or at a specific time chosen by the GM

A group that understands the rhythm of advancement usually finds it easier to stay invested in the campaign's pace.

## Choosing the Feel of Advancement

Leveling method affects the feel of play.

XP often makes advancement feel earned step by step. Every challenge adds to the total, and even a difficult session with no major story breakthrough can still move the party forward.

Milestones often make advancement feel dramatic and well-timed. Characters grow when the campaign reaches a meaningful turning point, which can make each new level land with extra weight.

Neither feeling is better. They simply create different rhythms.

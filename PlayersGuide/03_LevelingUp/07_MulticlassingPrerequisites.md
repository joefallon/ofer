## Multiclassing Prerequisites

Multiclassing opens new paths, but not every character can step onto every path at any time.

To gain a level in a new class, you must meet the ability score prerequisites for both your current class and the new class you want to enter. These minimum scores represent the training, discipline, talent, or natural aptitude needed to balance more than one class effectively.

In short, multiclassing is not just a matter of choice. Your character must be capable of supporting both paths.

## Why Prerequisites Exist

A beginning character receives the full foundation of training, instincts, and preparation that comes with starting in a class.

A multiclass character is doing something harder. You are adding a new set of techniques, disciplines, or magical practices on top of the ones you already have. The prerequisite scores reflect that extra demand.

A barbarian who wants to become a druid needs the strength to remain an effective barbarian and the wisdom to begin druidic study. A wizard who wants to become a fighter needs the intellect to continue as a wizard and the physical aptitude to function as a fighter.

## The Core Rule

To qualify for a new class, you must meet:

- the ability score minimum for your current class
- the ability score minimum for the new class

If you do not meet both, you cannot take a level in that new class.

## Multiclassing Prerequisites Table

|Class|Ability Score Minimum|
|---|---|
|Barbarian|STR 13|
|Bard|CHA 13|
|Cleric|WIS 13|
|Druid|WIS 13|
|Fighter|STR 13 or DEX 13|
|Mechanist|INT 13|
|Monk|DEX 13 and WIS 13|
|Paladin|STR 13 and CHA 13|
|Ranger|STR 13 or DEX 13 and WIS 13|
|Rogue|DEX 13|
|Sorcerer|CHA 13|
|Warlock|CHA 13|
|Wizard|INT 13|

## How to Read the Table

Most classes require one ability score of 13 or higher.

Some classes give you a choice. Fighter, for example, requires either Strength 13 or Dexterity 13. Ranger requires either Strength 13 or Dexterity 13, and also Wisdom 13.

Some classes require more than one score. A paladin needs both Strength 13 and Charisma 13. A monk needs both Dexterity 13 and Wisdom 13.

If a class entry says "or," meeting either listed option is enough. If it says "and," you must meet all listed requirements.

## Example: Barbarian to Druid

Roth is a barbarian with Strength 16 and Wisdom 13.

To multiclass into druid, Roth must meet the prerequisite for barbarian and the prerequisite for druid.

- Barbarian requires STR 13
- Druid requires WIS 13

Roth meets both requirements, so Roth can take a level in druid.

## Example: Fighter to Rogue

Sel has been playing a fighter and wants to take a rogue level.

Sel has Strength 14, Dexterity 12, and Charisma 10.

To multiclass, Sel must meet the fighter prerequisite and the rogue prerequisite.

- Fighter requires STR 13 or DEX 13
- Rogue requires DEX 13

Sel qualifies as a fighter because Strength is high enough, but does not qualify for rogue because Dexterity is only 12.

Sel cannot take a rogue level yet.

## Example: Paladin to Sorcerer

Iria is a paladin with Strength 13 and Charisma 14.

To multiclass into sorcerer, Iria must meet the paladin prerequisite and the sorcerer prerequisite.

- Paladin requires STR 13 and CHA 13
- Sorcerer requires CHA 13

Iria meets all of these requirements, so multiclassing into sorcerer is allowed.

## A Good Planning Habit

If you think you may want to multiclass later, keep the prerequisites in mind from the beginning of character creation.

A character who barely qualifies for one class may find some multiclass options closed off. A character built with a future multiclass in mind can enter that second class more smoothly when the time comes.

This does not mean every character should plan ahead. It simply means that multiclassing works best when it is an intentional choice rather than a surprise at the moment of leveling.

## Prerequisites Do Not Grant Features

Meeting a prerequisite only allows you to enter the class.

It does not give you any of that class's abilities by itself. You still gain those benefits only when you actually take a level in the class.

## Quick Reference

To take a level in a new class:

- check the prerequisite for your current class
- check the prerequisite for the new class
- meet both requirements
- if you do, you can take the new class level when you level up

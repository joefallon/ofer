## Why each candidate made the cut

### Alert

Very familiar, very easy to understand, very useful.  
Excellent fit for **Guard** and also broadly welcome in fantasy adventuring.

### Athlete

Simple physical competence feat.  
Fits **Sailor** especially well: climbing, balance, swimming, hauling, rough physical life.

### Combat Training

This is my recommended soldier feat instead of using something like _Savage Attacker_ as the default.  
It fits **Soldier** better, reads more clearly, and gives OFER freedom to design a more general martial-starter feat.

### Covert

Clean fit for **Criminal**.  
Lets OFER cover stealth, concealment, infiltration, shady habits, and quiet movement without needing to lean on a more advanced-feeling feat like _Skulker_.

### Crafter

Obvious inclusion.  
Excellent fit for **Artisan** and one of the most comfortable modern feat concepts.

### Healer

Strong, intuitive, useful, and low-friction.  
Best fit for **Hermit** in this first pass, especially if Hermit leans toward herbalism, field care, contemplation, and practical survival.

### Lucky

One of the best “new player fun” feats in concept.  
Excellent fit for **Urchin** as a scrappy survivor background.

### Magic Initiate

Locked in already for **Acolyte**.  
This is the correct comfort-first choice.

### Musician

Very clean fit for **Entertainer**.  
Familiar and easy to understand.

### Scholar

Important inclusion.  
**Sage** really wants a feat that says “study, learning, texts, memory, language, research, knowledge.”  
_Skilled_ is too broad to carry that whole identity by itself.

### Silver Tongue

Best fit for **Charlatan**.  
More precise than just giving Charlatan Lucky or Skilled.  
Covers deception, manipulation, fast talk, false confidence, and social slipperiness.

### Skilled

Very familiar, very useful, very beginner-friendly.  
Best fit for **Noble** in this first pass because noble upbringing often implies broad education, polish, training, and cultivated competence.

### Tough

Excellent fit for **Farmer**.  
This is one of the strongest background-feat pairings conceptually: hard work, endurance, resilience, practical grit.

### Trade Skills

Needed because **Merchant** deserves a feat that is not just generic charm or generic crafting.  
Merchant should feel like commerce, negotiation, valuation, connections, and practical market knowledge.

### Wayfinder

Excellent fit for **Wayfarer**.  
This is a very strong OFER-native pairing and gives the travel/wilderness/roadwise niche a clear home.

## Important naming note

I think this list also handles your IP/copyright concern well.

The safest and best OFER approach is:

- keep **generic, high-comfort names** where they help players
    
- use **original mechanics**
    
- use open text where available and useful
    
- use closed material only as inspiration for gameplay niche
    
- rename only when it improves clarity or avoids overly specific upstream expression
    

That is exactly what this list does.

## Design rules I would lock for Origin Feats

Before writing them, I would also lock these rules:

1. **Every Origin Feat must be easy to understand at level 1.**
    
2. **Every Origin Feat should fit in a short entry.**
    
3. **No Origin Feat should require complex build planning.**
    
4. **No Fighting Styles in the Origin Feat layer.**
    
5. **Each Origin Feat should express one strong fantasy clearly.**
    
6. **Origin Feats should avoid heavy conditional math.**
    
7. **Background first, optimization second.**
    

Those rules will make the whole chapter cleaner.
